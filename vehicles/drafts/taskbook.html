Driver Trainee Process and Task Book

Vehicles Department · vehicles@hobokenems.com
Companion to the HVAC Driver Training Manual and the EVOC reference guide · Last updated: 2026-06-02
Proposed draft – for FTO review. The level structure reflects Vehicles Department decisions. The skill checks and pass criteria below are a proposed starting point drawn from the Driver Training Manual; FTOs should confirm, add, or adjust them before this is adopted.
What this document is. This is the shared reference for driver trainees and Field Training Officers (FTOs). It defines the progression, the skill checks at each level, and the criteria for passing them, so that every FTO evaluates against the same bar and every trainee knows what is being assessed. It is not a sign-off sheet. Each evaluation is recorded by the FTO in the FTO Trainee Report form, and the trainee's current level and cleared status are tracked on the roster. Those records are kept separately, not in this document. The Driver Training Manual remains canonical for the gating logic; this reference restates only enough of it to be usable in the field.

1. How the progression works

Driver training is a graduated progression through three levels of increasing independence. A trainee spends a minimum of three shifts at each level. Advancement through Levels 1 and 2 may be cleared by any FTO; advancement to full driver status requires a recommendation from two FTOs and clearance by the Vehicles Lt or Sgt.

Level Permits Supervision Advancement
Level 1 Non-emergent driving (no lights or sirens) FTO in the front seat After a minimum of three shifts, any FTO may clear the trainee to Level 2
Level 2 Emergent driving (lights and sirens) FTO in the front seat After a minimum of three shifts, any FTO may clear the trainee to Level 3
Level 3 Emergent driving (lights and sirens) FTO anywhere in the ambulance After a minimum of three shifts and a recommendation from two FTOs, the Vehicles Lt or Sgt may clear the trainee to full driver status

2. Evaluation standard

To keep clearance consistent across FTOs, every check below is judged by the same standard:

  • A check is met when the trainee performs it correctly and unprompted, with no FTO intervention required for safety.
  • A check observed only once is not enough; it should be met consistently across the level's shifts before advancement.
  • The FTO records the result of each shift in the FTO Trainee Report form.

3. Entry prerequisites (before Level 1)

A trainee may begin Level 1 once all of the following are complete:

  • All onboarding paperwork submitted and reviewed, including a copy of the driver's license and a current driving abstract.
  • Holds a valid, current driver's license.
  • Passed the written quiz on the Driver Training Manual and the EVOC reference guide (see quiz scope below).
  • Cleared on the rig check and on the location of all ambulance contents. It is the trainee's responsibility to learn each rig's supplies and layout using the rig-check forms.

4. Quiz scope

The entry quiz is a short written test covering the material a trainee must know before driving. It draws from:

  • Driver Training Manual: general driving policies, the law (no exemptions, no speeding, no wrong-way driving, school buses, railroad crossings), response types, security mode, on-scene and transport procedures, and vehicle-contact reporting.
  • EVOC reference guide: due regard, the cushion of safety, speed and stopping distance, intersections, backing, lights and sirens, and the effects of fatigue and distraction.
To set. The pass threshold and whether the quiz is delivered on paper or as a Microsoft Forms quiz.

5. Level 1 skill checks Non-emergent

Level 1 establishes basic vehicle handling and shift procedures under direct supervision, with the FTO in the front seat.

  • Pre-shift readiness: signs in on the duty shift iPad; completes the rig check; performs the correct startup sequence; exits the bay with headlights and emergency lights on, checking both directions for pedestrians and traffic.
  • Security mode: activates and deactivates it correctly on the rig in use.
  • Non-emergent road operation: holds lane position, following distance, and a cushion of safety; brakes and accelerates smoothly; comes to full stops and scans lane by lane at intersections; keeps within the speed limit.
  • Backing and return to base: backs only with a visible spotter and stops the moment the spotter leaves view; reverses into the bay per procedure; connects the shoreline.
  • Turning and overhang: accounts for tail swing and checks the appropriate rear corner before turning.
  • End of shift: returns the D-1 radio to charge, leaves fuel at or above half a tank, and leaves the vehicle clean.

6. Level 2 skill checks Emergent

Level 2 adds emergency-mode operation, still with the FTO in the front seat.

  • Per-rig controls competency (gates entry from Level 1): correctly operates the emergency lights, the siren including tone changes, and the radio on each rig, accounting for the differences between units – for example, 922's electronic push-buttons versus the manual switches on other trucks.
  • Emergent response driving: uses lights and sirens appropriately and with due regard; does not exceed the speed limit; obeys all law, including no wrong-way driving, school buses, and railroad crossings.
  • Intersections under emergency conditions: slows or stops, clears the intersection lane by lane, changes the siren tone on approach, and proceeds only when traffic has yielded.
  • Response-type adaptation: confirms the response type with the crew chief and adapts if it changes en route.
  • On scene: positions the vehicle for crew and scene safety; engages security mode; switches emergency lights to secondary; directs crew egress accounting for traffic, bicycles, and scooters.
  • Emergent transport: drives smoothly enough for the crew to work, and selects the correct destination bay.

7. Level 3 and full clearance Emergent

Level 3 confirms the trainee can operate independently, with the FTO no longer in the front seat.

  • Independent operation: performs all Level 1 and Level 2 competencies with no safety-critical intervention while the FTO is elsewhere in the ambulance.
  • Judgment under load: manages the unexpected – sudden stops, uncooperative traffic, weather, and congestion – and declines unsafe maneuvers regardless of lights and sirens.
  • Full-shift competence: completes a full shift's driving duties, end to end, without prompting.

Clearance to full driver: two FTOs independently observe the trainee and recommend clearance; the Vehicles Lt or Sgt then grants full driver status.

8. Recording and clearance

  • The FTO records each shift's evaluation in the FTO Trainee Report form.
  • The roster reflects the trainee's current level and cleared status.
  • Advancement requires both the minimum of three shifts at the level and the checks for that level met to standard.
  • This document defines the checks; it does not hold any individual's records.
Coupling note. The FTO Trainee Report form should be rebuilt to capture exactly the checks listed here, so that FTOs evaluate and record against the same structure.

9. Re-clearance after an incident

Driver status is not permanent. As stated in the Driver Training Manual, any at-fault or significant vehicle contact suspends a driver's status pending review. Return to driving requires re-clearance by the Vehicles Lt or Sgt, which may include a drop to a supervised level – a return to Level 2 or Level 1 under an FTO – and re-completion of the relevant checks above.
