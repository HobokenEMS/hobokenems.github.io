Overview

First and foremost, this isn't just a training manual for drivers - it's intended as a reference for all HVAC members, since everyone should know the basics about the vehicles they're in. Awareness is key. This training manual is divided into three modules:

  1. Introduction to Vehicles: Required for all members.
  2. Introduction to Emergency Vehicles: Required for all members with a driver's license.
  3. Emergency Vehicle Operations: Required for all driver trainees.

As members get closer to driver training, they progress through the different modules. This style is necessary because non-driver members may be called upon to drive other vehicles (such as the ALS flycar/ambulance or supervisor's vehicle) in urgent situations. All members with a driver's license should be, at minimum, capable of operating emergency vehicles in a non-emergency mode safely and expeditiously.

Section I: Introduction to Vehicles

  • Truck Familiarization: (including the differences between ambulances)
  • Policies: Lights & sirens, ALS vehicles, licensed vs. certified ambulances
  • Warning Devices: Cold/warm/hot standardization, other generic information

Section II: Introduction to Emergency Vehicles

  • Definitions: Laws governing emergency vehicles, translations to real-life operations, permissible actions while operating in emergency mode
  • Sterile Response: No unrelated conversations, how to be a good passenger, navigating Hoboken (directions/city layout)
  • Warning Devices: Appropriate use of warning devices (transporting, responding, utility purposes), safety requirements
    • Why? EMTs are expected to determine if the patient needs lights and sirens transport. Drivers are expected to assess environmental/operational conditions (weather, roads, traffic, distance) and determine if honoring the EMT's directive would be safe to do.
